Who We Are

The Garnet Fund

Garnet Fund is a student-managed investment fund at the Darla Moore School of Business. Founded in 2024, over 35 undergraduate students manage a real equity and alternatives portfolio each fall. This year we are starting with $110,000 under management, with a five-year goal of reaching $1 million in AUM. Our purpose is simple: give USC students hands-on experience with the skills that matter in finance.

Members go through a structured curriculum covering financial modeling, stock valuation, and portfolio management, and compete in investment competitions throughout the year. As the fund grows, we plan to put a portion of returns back into the school through scholarships and educational resources, subject to approval from our advisory board and faculty advisors.

Investment Philosophy

How We Invest

Garnet Fund manages risk by diversifying across a long/short equity portfolio, taking long positions in companies we believe are undervalued and short positions in those we consider overvalued. Analysts assess individual stocks and pitch ideas based on fundamental analysis and valuation, with the goal of generating returns from both rising and falling markets while managing overall portfolio risk.

Investment Strategy

Long / Short, Bottom-Up

We follow a long/short, bottom-up investment strategy where students research individual companies and pitch them based on fundamental analysis and valuation. This approach aims to generate returns from both sides of the market while reducing overall exposure.

Investable Capital

Capital Allocation

Of our total allocated capital, ~75% is managed by the long/short equity team and the remaining ~25% is allocated to the alternatives team. Investments are focused on U.S. public equities across sectors covered by the S&P 1000.

Defense

Investment Committee Review

All investment pitches are submitted to the Investment Committee for evaluation. During weekly meetings, ideas are stress-tested by the full team. A majority vote from the committee is required before any position is entered.

Due Diligence

Position Sizing & Monitoring

Portfolio managers work closely with sector teams to determine position sizing, taking into account current exposure and diversification. Positions are continuously monitored to assess whether the original thesis remains valid.

Analyst Training

Six-Week Training Program

Every new analyst goes through a six-week training program before touching the live portfolio. It covers accounting, financial modeling, and investment research, and by the end, members are ready to pitch a real stock idea to the full fund.

Week 1

Introduction to the Fund & Value Investing

Overview of the fund, meet your onboarding class, and an introduction to the principles of value investing.

Week 2

Accounting, Ratios & Multiples

A deep dive into accounting fundamentals, key financial ratios, and valuation multiples. We cover what they are, how to read them, and when they actually matter in practice.

Week 3

Financial Modeling

Analysts build a full three-statement model from scratch, then work through DCF, comps, precedent transactions, and a reverse DCF, all applied to the same company from Week 2.

Week 4

Initial Research & Idea Generation

Learn how to source investment ideas, develop an initial thesis, and structure early-stage research on a company.

Week 5

Buyside Modeling: Levers & Drivers

Go beyond accounting models to understand implied levers, value drivers, and how buyside investors frame an investment.

Week 6

Buyside Modeling: Data & Forecasting

Learn how to use data to forecast business performance and pressure-test your model assumptions.

Member Benefits

What You'll Gain

01

Live Portfolio Management

Members contribute directly to a live long/short equity portfolio. Your research drives actual investment decisions. You pitch ideas, defend them in front of the full team, track how positions perform, and get a real sense of what accountability looks like in a professional setting.

02

Continuous Learning

The learning does not stop after the training program. Senior analysts work closely with newer members, the market constantly creates new things to think through, and the curriculum gets updated to reflect what finance professionals actually want to see from candidates today.

03

Career Placement

Garnet Fund alumni have landed roles at Morgan Stanley, Bank of America, Evercore, Point72, and others. The combination of technical training, real portfolio experience, and a strong alumni network makes a genuine difference when recruiting season comes around.
